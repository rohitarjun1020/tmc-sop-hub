SOP 01

P&L Proforma Forecasting

Standard Operating Procedure for building and maintaining TMC's monthly Profit & Loss forecast

Document TitleP&L Pro Forma Forecasting SOP
CompanyThe Migration Company
DepartmentFinance / FP&A

Purpose

This SOP establishes a standardized methodology for preparing, maintaining, updating, and reviewing the Profit & Loss (P&L) Pro Forma Forecast Model.

  • Consistent forecasting methodology
  • Reliable financial projections
  • Transparency of assumptions
  • Improved decision making
  • Standardized reporting across stakeholders

Scope & Stakeholders

This SOP applies to all teams involved in financial planning and analysis:

  • Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A)
  • Finance Team
  • Management Team
  • Department Heads (operational input)
  • External Consultants (forecasting support)
Key Forecast Drivers

Revenue Drivers

  • B2B Revenue Language Training Courses
  • RTD Revenue Recruitment Training & Deployment

Cost Drivers (COGS)

  • B2B COGS Teacher Consultants
  • RTD COGS Channel Partners, Sourcing Companies

Operating Expense Drivers

  • Operational Salaries
  • OPEX — G&A + S&M
  • Technology Subscriptions
  • Other G&A Overheads
  • Professional Charges
P&L Structure — How the Numbers Flow
Sheet-Wise Preparation

Revenue Buildup Sheet

The first sheet to build. It captures all revenue generated in a particular month.

1
Source Data: Pull batch info from Operational MIS → 2026 Batches sheet — batch name, client, start/end dates, learner count, fees.
2
Filter Active Courses: Include only batches ending after the current month's start date to capture ongoing + starting courses.
3
RTD Revenue: Include RTD Pipeline revenue — ensure corresponding COGS is also captured in COGS sheet.
4
Working Days Calculation: Use =NETWORKDAYS(start, end) for total course workdays.
5
Monthly Proration: Month Workdays ÷ Total Course Workdays = % of Revenue for the Month
6
Currency: If fees are in USD, convert to INR using the prevailing rate.
Revenue Proration Formula
Month Workdays
÷
Total Course Workdays
×
Total Course Fee
= Monthly Revenue
Key Note: Only payroll teachers are excluded from COGS — their cost is captured in Operational Salaries instead.

COGS Buildup Sheet

All courses from the Revenue sheet appear here too — but payroll teachers are excluded from COGS as their cost is in Operational Salaries.

1
Data Source: Get teacher name, course, teaching hours and cost/hour from Operational MIS → Teachers sheet.
2
Monthly Teaching Hours: Teaching Hours ÷ Total Workdays × Month Workdays
3
Monthly COGS: Monthly Teaching Hours × Cost Per Hour
4
Remember: Payroll teachers' cost goes to Manpower sheet as Operational Salaries, not here.
COGS Calculation
Teaching Hours (course)
÷
Total Workdays
×
Month Workdays
×
Cost Per Hour
= Monthly COGS

Manpower Sheet

Covers payroll for all TMC employees across three categories. Data sourced from TMC Payroll FY 26-27 Google Sheet.

Operations Salaries

Teachers on payroll — cost NOT in COGS

G&A Salaries

Management & admin staff salaries

S&M Salaries

Sales & marketing team salaries

Data Source: TMC Payroll FY 26-27 Google Sheet on TMC Drive.

Expenses Sheet

Divided into 3 main expense categories. Most figures are sourced from the Cashflow Forecast file.

Professional Charges — Assumed based on the cash flow data
S&M Charges — Software, travel, other S&M expenses (from cashflow)
G&A Overheads — Courier, telephone, printing, internet, office rent, misc (from cashflow)

Final P&L Sheet

Consolidates all prior sheets into the complete Profit & Loss statement.

B2B Revenue + RTD RevenueRevenue
— B2B COGS — RTD COGSDeduct
= Contribution Margin
— Operational SalariesDeduct
= Gross Profit
— OPEX (S&M + G&A)Deduct
= EBITDA
Total Burn = COGS + Op. Salaries + Total OPEX
EBITDA is the primary management metric. Total Burn tracks the cash consumed each month.

Executive Summary

The TMC Cash Flow Forecasting Model provides management with visibility into future cash movements, liquidity position, operational cash requirements, burn rate, and cash runway. The model consolidates operational, payroll, recruitment, sales, and overhead data to forecast monthly cash inflows and outflows — visualized through a Looker Studio dashboard.

Forecast monthly cash position Monitor liquidity availability Track cash burn & runway Support budgeting & planning Support management decision-making

Sales Pipeline

Sheet-Wise Preparation

Payroll Sheet

Contains all TMC employees across 3 categories with forecasted salaries through March 2027.

Operations

Teaching staff

G&A

Management team

S&M

Sales & Marketing

Critical Note — Salary Timing: Salaries are paid in the same month only if the month-end is a working day. If the last day is a weekend or public holiday, salaries are paid on the first working day of the next month.

Example: May's salary was credited in June because the last working day of May fell on a Sunday.
Verification: Cross-check salary amounts against bank statements AND the TMC Payroll Google Sheet.

Consultant Sheet

All G&A and S&M consultants of TMC, including legal fee arrangements.

1
Includes all consultancy costs for G&A and S&M departments
2
Legal fees are included — review contracts for amounts and timing
Sahil (Consultant) — Special Rule: Sahil is paid ₹10,000/month as consultant fees. Any additional amount credited to him is TDS — this must be accounted separately in the Expense Breakup sheet, not here.

Reimbursement Sheet

All employee reimbursements categorized and tracked here.

1
Reimbursement amounts found in TMC Payroll Google Sheet with attached invoices
2
Open each invoice and categorize: food, travel, internet, etc.
3
Categorized items feed into the Expense Breakup sheet
Claude Tip — Large Invoice Volumes: If there are many invoices, download them all to a folder, give Claude folder access + reimbursement sheet. Claude will categorize all invoices into existing categories.

Always recheck the total Claude gives against the reimbursement sheet total.

B2B Revenue Sheet

Language training revenue — two sections: Confirmed and Pipeline.

Key contacts: Ms. Nikhitha Joshi & Mr. Sidhant

C
Confirmed Revenue: Active courses. Dates and course data from Operational MIS. Teacher allocated by TMC (visible in Teachers sheet).
P
Pipeline Revenue: Expected future batches. Assumption: 60% conversion rate from pipeline to confirmed.
B2B Revenue Formula
=15 × 270 × 50% × 93
15 = Batch size (students) 270 = Fee per student (USD) 50% = First payment (50% upfront) 93 = USD → INR conversion
Payment Models
Monthly Payment
Consultant teachers paid monthly. Formula: =16 × 1000 × 90% (hours × rate × 90% after 10% TDS)
50-50 Model
50% on course start, 50% on completion. Both revenue receipt and cost payment follow this model.

RTD Revenue Sheet

Recruitment, Training & Deployment — two sections: Confirmed and Pipeline.

Key contact: Ms. Priyansha

RTD Revenue Formula
=8 × 100,000 × 50%
8 = Number of nurses required by client ₹1,00,000 = Fee charged per nurse 50% = First payment (50% upfront when process starts)
RTD Costs: Consultant costs associated with RTD courses (including pipeline costs) are captured in the RTD Payments sheet.

Main Cashflow Sheet

The consolidated cashflow statement. Top-left toggle to switch between With Pipeline or Without Pipeline scenarios.

Opening Balance (= Prior Month Closing Balance)
+ Receipts Investments + B2B Revenue + RTD Collections + Other Income (interest)
− Payments B2B Costs + RTD Costs + Payroll + Travel + Software + G&A + Telecom + Courier + Consultants + Printing + Professional/Legal
Closing Balance = Opening + Inflows − Outflows ± Adjustments
Check Row Closing Balance − Bank Balance = Must be ZERO
Gross Burn = Total Outflows
Net Burn = Opening Balance − Closing Balance
